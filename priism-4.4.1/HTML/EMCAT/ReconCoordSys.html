Coordinate System for EM Tomography Reconstructions

Priism's applications for reconstructing EM tomograms use this relationship between a coordinate, (xv, yv, zv), in the reconstruction volume and its projection, (xp, yp), to the aligned coordinate grid for the tilt angle, ta: 

    [ xp ]   [ cos(ta)  0  sin(ta) ] [ xv-0.5*(nxv-1) ]   [ 0.5*(nxp-1) ]
    [    ] = [                     ] [       yv       ] + [             ]
    [ yp ]   [    0     1     0    ] [ zv-0.5*(nxv-1) ]   [      0      ]

nxv and nzv are the number of samples along the x and z axes, respectively, of the reconstruction. nxp is the number of samples along the x axis of the aligned projection.

All of Priism's applications for reconstructing EM tomograms assume the projections have been aligned such that the tilt axis is parallel to the y axis. The section titled, Apply Alignment, of APPL_PRM.html describes the coordinate transformations used to map the unaligned projections to the aligned coordinate system.

Given two planes, z = z1 and z = z2, with z1 greater than z2 from the reconstruction of a sample measured with an underfocus, the convention for the change in defocus used by Priism's CTF correction application, pfocusramp, implies that the plane at z1 had less of an underfocus at zero tilt than the plane at z2.
